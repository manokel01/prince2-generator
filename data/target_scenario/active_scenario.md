# Scenario 2: Louistown City Council

Louistown is a historical town which is home to over 40,000 residents and attracts over 20,000 visitors per year. The city council has been criticized for the lack of investment and modernization of the town centre.

They have recently decided to improve the vibrancy of the area and offer increased opportunities for business by approving the funds for the **'Louistown is Open for Business' (LOB)** portfolio. This is an ambitious portfolio of projects covering the next five-year plan cycle.

A new shopping centre, provisionally named **'LouisShopping'**, is seen as a key project within this portfolio. This is a complex infrastructure project due to the:

- location of the building and historical nature of the surrounding buildings
- requirement for the design to be consistent with the environment while offering modern facilities supported by the latest technology
- need to include underground parking space
- importance of minimizing disruption to residents and businesses during the construction works
- recent award of **'green town' status** to Louistown, and the sustainability requirements this will impose on the development

Due to the nature of the work, in which just the construction element is likely to span over two financial years, the council has approved the strategic business case, the initial high-level project plan and has appointed **BuildyBrick** as the main contractor.

They have established a requirement for a traditional delivery method in line with their standard approach to projects. The general view is that this project is long overdue, and the council would like 'LouisShopping' to be open to the public by the end of financial year three.

The project has just completed the initiation stage and commenced the first delivery stage (stage 2). The high-level project plan is as follows:

---

## High-Level Project Plan

| Stage | Activities |
|-------|-----------|
| **Stage 1 — Initiation** *(complete)* | Strategic business case and high-level project plan approval; appoint contractor |
| **Stage 2** *(in progress)* | Design |
| **Stage 3** | Site preparation; utilities and systems; construction |
| **Stage 4** | Testing; inspections |
| **Stage 5** | Demobilisation |

The main output from the project is a shopping centre constructed and ready to open to the public.

## Additional Information

### Roles

**Portfolio Director**
A senior council official overseeing the entire 'Louistown is Open for Business' five-year investment plan. They view LouisShopping as the portfolio's anchor project and control the release of funds across all portfolio stages.

**Business Opportunities Director**
A council officer focused on the long-term economic impact of the regeneration programme. After project closure, they are responsible for assessing whether the shopping centre has increased commercial activity and attracted new businesses to Louistown.

**Head of Regeneration**
A senior Louistown City Council official with ultimate accountability for the LouisShopping project. They ensure the project delivers value for taxpayers and remains aligned with the council's strategic regeneration goals.

**Chair, Louistown Business Association**
The elected representative of local business owners and retailers. They advocate for the interests of existing businesses during construction and for the commercial viability of the completed shopping centre.

**Contracts Director, BuildyBrick**
The senior BuildyBrick representative attending project oversight meetings. They advocate for the contractor's commercial interests, oversee contractual compliance, and present delivery progress updates on behalf of the construction firm.

**Project Manager**
The day-to-day coordinator of the LouisShopping project, responsible for drafting management approaches, orchestrating stakeholder workshops, and monitoring progress against the agreed stage plan. They escalate issues and risks that fall outside agreed tolerances to the oversight committee.

**Project Support Officer**
Responsible for maintaining the administrative backbone of the project, including version control of project records and the distribution of approved documentation to the relevant teams. Following a lesson learned from an outdated floor plan incident, they became the sole authorised distributor of all project records.

**Heritage Director**
A specialist council officer with authority over the preservation of Louistown's historical buildings and environment. They were centrally involved in tense discussions about the acceptance criteria for the shopping centre design, ensuring the build remains consistent with the town's heritage character.

**Sustainability Manager**
A council expert responsible for ensuring the LouisShopping project meets Louistown's 'green town' commitments and carbon emission limits. They advise on sustainable design choices and monitor compliance with the council's net zero pledge throughout the project.

**Architect**
A design specialist responsible for producing a shopping centre design that is consistent with the historical environment while incorporating modern facilities and technology. They participated in the acceptance criteria workshops alongside the Heritage Director and Sustainability Manager.

**Principal Electrical Building Services Engineer**
A specialist from the main contractor leading the electrical team and managing all work packages related to power distribution and building services. Their team experienced a week of rework after receiving an outdated version of the building floor plan.

**Maintenance Plumber**
An experienced contractor from the construction firm coordinating all plumbing and drainage work packages. During site surveys, their team's excavations uncovered an old pipe in an unexpected location, triggering a formal issue to be raised and assessed for its impact on the project schedule.

**BuildyBrick Team Leaders**
Specialist supervisors from the main contractor responsible for directing on-site construction teams across multiple work streams. They attend regular health and safety meetings with the project coordinator as a control measure given the proximity of historical buildings.

**Independent Contractors**
External specialists with no contractual ties to the main construction firm. They carry out planned site inspections throughout the construction stages to provide independent assurance on safety and compliance.

**Mall Federation Representatives**
Officials from a national industry standards body whose latest construction standards have been formally adopted by both Louistown City Council and the main contractor. Their standards are referenced in quality management documentation and form the benchmark criteria used during inspections and audits.
